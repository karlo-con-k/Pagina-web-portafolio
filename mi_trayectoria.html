My career

I started my academic journey in high school. Where I chose to study mathematics in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Guanajuato, or DEMAT. However, I did not expect that the first year and a half of the degree would be the most difficult of my life.

When I started at DEMAT, I used to study around 5 hours for day. But I didn't understand the class topics, while my classmates easily mastered the subjects. Even with increasing my efforts, I couldn't pass my courses. There is nothing that has hurt me more in life than giving it my all and fail.

When I repeated my first semester courses. I decided that I would succeed or I would change my degree. Throughout the semester, I woke up at 7am to go to class, finished at 2pm my class, ate lunch and walked from 2pm to 3pm until I arrived at the cafe where I studied from 3pm to 12:00am, and then walked and ate on my way back home. Whenever I wanted to take a break, I read books on how to solve math problems or how to make proofs. I only rested on Friday nights. Thanks to that immense effort, I managed to pass my courses. Afterwards, each semester became easier than the previous one. Although I continued seeking challenges, such as taking master's level courses while in my fourth semester or attending summer schools.

I decided to focus on Computer Science throughout my career. I took around 50% of the courses for the Master's degree at CIMAT. Even though it would have been more comfortable for me to pursue a Master's degree in Computer Science, I decided to step out of my comfort zone and look for a job in that field.
